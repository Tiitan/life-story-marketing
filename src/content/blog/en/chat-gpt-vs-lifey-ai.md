A recent viral Reddit post is a painful reminder that general-purpose AI can be the wrong tool for something as personal as a book about your life. According to the Yahoo Tech report, one user spent 16 days believing ChatGPT was creating a 700-page illustrated children's book, only to find that nothing was actually ready to download.

The intention was heartfelt. The user had spent months adapting stories from Indian mythology and wanted to turn them into a meaningful gift for children in their community. But the experience exposed a bigger problem: chatbots can sound confident and helpful even when they do not have the memory, structure, or long-term reliability needed for a real book project.

## The Core Problem: AI Without Memory

That story highlights the biggest weakness of tools like ChatGPT for memoirs, family histories, and other deeply personal writing projects. They do not carry a true long-term memory of your story.

Each new conversation starts fresh. The AI does not remember the first chapter you drafted last week, the childhood detail you shared in a previous session, or the emotional thread that ties your stories together. For a life story, that is not a small inconvenience. It is a dealbreaker.

General-purpose chatbots are designed to be eager and fluent. That can be useful for quick tasks, but it can also create a dangerous illusion: the feeling that progress is happening when the tool is really just generating reassuring language.
